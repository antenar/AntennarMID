How It Works

Antenna Integration

We use Laser Direct Structuring to create copper antenna traces directly on your 3D parts. The part's surface becomes the antenna, maximizing collection area and RF gain compared to external antennas.

RF-DC Conversion

Integrated circuits convert collected RF energy to DC power, charging the device's battery. The RF-DC conversion efficiency scales with antenna surface area and optimal impedance matching.

Why This Works Now: Koomey's Law

Koomey's Law states that the energy efficiency of computation doubles every 1.57 years. Since 1945, compute energy requirements have decreased by a factor of 100 billion. Modern electronics consume orders of magnitude less power than previous generations, creating a convergence point where RF-DC power output can meet device power requirements.

Three Manufacturing Categories

OEMs

Device enclosures with integrated RF harvesting

We manufacture enclosures, cases, and structural components where the part itself functions as both mechanical housing and RF collection antenna. The collected energy charges the device's internal battery through integrated RF-DC conversion circuits.

  • Laptop cases with battery charging capability
  • Smartphone shells with wireless power collection
  • IoT sensor housings with continuous power
  • Industrial monitoring device enclosures

Batteries

Battery enclosures as RF collection surfaces

The battery case becomes the antenna system. This maximizes available surface area for RF collection while protecting battery cells. The case structure harvests RF energy to continuously charge the enclosed batteries.

  • Self-charging battery packs
  • Wireless power banks
  • Continuous IoT power modules
  • Emergency backup power systems

Solar PV Films

Solar panels with integrated RF collection

We print MID antenna patterns directly onto solar panel films using our process technology. The panels harvest both photovoltaic and RF energy simultaneously. The RF antenna traces are integrated within the solar cell's existing copper interconnect structure.

  • Solar panels with RF energy collection
  • Indoor energy harvesting films
  • Flexible dual-mode power films
  • Building-integrated energy systems
